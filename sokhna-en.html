Ain Sokhna — Complete Overview

(1) Location & Distances

  • On the Gulf of Suez (Red Sea), south of Suez City.
  • Cairo → ~130–140 km (about 75–90 minutes depending on the road).
  • New Administrative Capital → ~60 minutes.
  • Suez City → ~50–60 km.

(2) Administration

Ain Sokhna belongs to Suez Governorate with convenient access to public services.

(3) Access & Main Roads

  • Cairo – Ain Sokhna direct highway.
  • Zafarana Road (links Galala resorts and the southern sector).
  • Galala Plateau Road (connects the mountain to coastal resorts).
  • Suez – Ain Sokhna Road.
  • Links to Hurghada/Ismailia axes for Red Sea cities.

(4) Weather & Best Time

  • Mild most of the year: cooler humidity than the North Coast in summer.
  • Peak times: weekends and year-round thanks to proximity to Cairo.

(5) Activities & Leisure

  • Water sports: swimming, snorkeling, fishing, boat trips.
  • Mountain experiences: Galala ascent and panoramic viewpoints.
  • Galala cable car (subject to operation), slides & aqua parks in some resorts.
  • Spas, health clubs, promenades, and cycling tracks in many compounds.

(6) Essentials & Medical

  • Pharmacies and medical services within most resorts and hotels.
  • Close to Suez hospitals, with emergency services along the roads.

(7) Key Areas & Corridors

  • Galala (upper/lower) — resorts, hotels, and leisure venues.
  • Early Zafarana strip — tiered compounds with wide sea views.
  • Zone after Ain Sokhna gates toward Porto & Gulf of Suez.

(8) Project Types

  • Chalets & apartments with sea/garden/lagoon views.
  • Standalone, twin & town villas in select projects.
  • 4–5 star hotels & serviced hospitality within compounds.

(9) Investment Edge

  • Closest Red Sea destination to Cairo → strong year-round occupancy.
  • Diverse payment plans, services, and maintenance.
  • Solid rental value during seasons & holidays.
