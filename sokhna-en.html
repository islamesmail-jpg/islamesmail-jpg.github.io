Ain Sokhna — Full Guide

(1) Location & Distances

  • On the Gulf of Suez (Red Sea), south of Suez City.
  • Cairo ~ 130–140 km (≈ 75–90 min depending on route).
  • New Administrative Capital ~ ≈ 60 min.
  • Suez City ~ 50–60 km.

(2) Administration

Ain Sokhna is under Suez Governorate with easy access to services.

(3) Access Roads

  • Cairo – Ain Sokhna direct road.
  • Zafarana Road (links Galala & southern strip).
  • Galala Plateau Road (connects the plateau with coastal resorts).
  • Suez – Ain Sokhna Road.
  • Hurghada/Ismailia axes (Red Sea connectivity).

(4) Climate & Best Time

  • Mild most of the year; lower humidity than the North Coast in summer.
  • Peak: weekends and year-round due to proximity to Cairo.

(5) Activities

  • Water sports: swimming, snorkeling, fishing, boat trips.
  • Mountain experiences: Galala viewpoints and drives.
  • Galala cable car (when operating), slides, aqua parks (in some resorts).
  • Spa, promenades, cycling tracks across many compounds.

(6) Essentials & Medical

  • Pharmacies and medical services across resorts and hotels.
  • Close to Suez hospitals; roadside emergency services available.

(7) Main Zones

  • Galala (upper/lower) — resorts, hotels, leisure spots.
  • Early Zafarana strip — graded compounds with sea facades/views.
  • Area after Ain Sokhna gates toward Porto & Gulf of Suez.

(8) Project Types

  • Chalets & apartments (sea/garden/lagoon views).
  • Standalone, twin, and town villas in select projects.
  • 4–5 star hotels & serviced residences within compounds.

(9) Investment Pros

  • Closest Red Sea destination to Cairo → high year-round occupancy.
  • Diverse payment & maintenance/service models.
  • Strong rental value in seasons and holidays.
